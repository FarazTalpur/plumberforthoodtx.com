Frequently Asked Questions

Low water pressure in your bathroom sink can be caused by several factors including clogged aerators, partially shut valves, or pipe blockages. Cleaning the aerator or checking the valves can often resolve the issue.

A toilet that keeps running is often due to a faulty flapper or a misadjusted float in the tank. Checking the flapper for wear and ensuring that the float is set at the correct level can fix this problem.

You can unclog a slow draining bathroom sink by removing any debris from the drain, using a plunger, or applying a mixture of baking soda followed by vinegar. For stubborn clogs, it may be necessary to manually remove the trap.

A leaking showerhead can often be fixed by tightening the connections or replacing worn-out rubber washers or seals. Sometimes, cleaning the showerhead to clear mineral build-up can also resolve the issue.

To prevent mold and mildew, ensure proper ventilation by using an exhaust fan or opening windows during and after showers. Regularly cleaning surfaces and fixing any leaks promptly also helps in reducing moisture build-up.
